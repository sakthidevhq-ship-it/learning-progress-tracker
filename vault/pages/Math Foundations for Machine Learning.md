## Summary
A curriculum module reviewing the calculus, linear algebra, and probability needed to understand ML algorithms, including gradients, matrix operations, and computational graphs for automatic differentiation.

## Key Takeaways
- Gradients generalize derivatives to multivariate functions and point in the direction of steepest ascent
- Matrix multiplication underlies nearly every layer operation in deep learning
- Computational graphs make automatic differentiation tractable by decomposing complex functions into traceable elementary operations
