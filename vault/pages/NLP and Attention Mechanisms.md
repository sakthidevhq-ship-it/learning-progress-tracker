## Summary
A curriculum module tracing the evolution of NLP techniques from tokenization and n-gram models through word embeddings to early attention mechanisms like Bahdanau and Luong attention in encoder-decoder architectures.

## Key Takeaways
- Word embeddings capture semantic similarity as geometric proximity in a learned vector space
- Bahdanau attention lets a decoder selectively focus on relevant encoder states rather than relying on a single fixed context vector
- Attention mechanisms directly motivated the fully attention-based Transformer architecture that followed
